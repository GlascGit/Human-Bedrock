# Sleep Bedrock – Extended

## SB1 – Prolonged total sleep deprivation is fatal in humans.

### What it means
“Total” means zero sleep—not just poor sleep, not just a few hours.
“Prolonged” means days to weeks. No human has ever survived complete,
long‑term absence of sleep.

### The strongest human evidence
Fatal familial insomnia (FFI) is a prion disease that destroys the
thalamus, the brain’s sleep gate. Patients first struggle to sleep,
then lose the ability entirely. Despite the best medical care, all FFI
patients die, typically within 6–30 months after onset. There is no
survivor on record.

### Animal evidence
Rats subjected to total sleep deprivation (disk‑over‑water method)
all died within 2–3 weeks. The cause of death was systemic collapse
(sepsis, metabolic failure), not a single organ failure. The
reproducibility of this across species makes the case overwhelming.

### What it does NOT say
This fact does not claim that sleeping less than 7 hours will kill you
quickly, nor that a few all‑nighters cause permanent damage. It only
states the outermost, non‑negotiable boundary: sleep is a biological
necessity, and its total absence is incompatible with life.

### Historical note
The universal human intuition that prolonged wakefulness kills or
causes madness is visible in ancient torture practices (e.g., Roman
*tormentum vigilae*) and in medieval law, long before any science
existed to confirm it. The empirical confirmation simply validated
what every culture already knew.
